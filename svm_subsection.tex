\subsubsection{Support Vector Machines (SVM)}

The Support Vector Machine was trained using a Radial Basis Function
(RBF) kernel, which is well suited for the non-linear separation of
spectral classes. The ten spectral bands (B2--B12) were standardized
(zero mean, unit variance) prior to training, since SVM is sensitive
to feature scaling. A regularization parameter of $C=10$ and
\texttt{gamma="scale"} were used, and the model was trained on 70\% of
the samples and evaluated on the remaining 30\% (2400 pixels).

\noindent\textbf{Overall Accuracy:} 85.96\%

\vspace{0.3cm}
\noindent The SVM achieved strong performance in distinguishing
\textit{Vegetaci\'on} (F1 = 0.947) and \textit{Zona urbana}
(F1 = 0.951), whose spectral signatures are clearly separable.
The main source of error was the mutual confusion between
\textit{Cultivos} and \textit{Suelo desnudo}, which share similar
reflectance values in several bands, reducing their individual
F1-scores to approximately 0.77.
