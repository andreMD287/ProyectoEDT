\section{Introduction}
%------------------------------------------------

The municipality of Armero, located in the department of Tolima, Colombia, was the site of one of the deadliest volcanic disasters in Latin America. On November 13, 1985, the eruption of the Nevado del Ruiz volcano generated lahars that buried much of the town, causing severe environmental and human losses.

This project aims to analyze the current land surface cover of the Armero region using Sentinel-2 multispectral satellite imagery and supervised machine learning techniques. Through spectral analysis and classification models, the study seeks to identify and quantify different land cover classes such as vegetation, water bodies, bare soil, and urban remnants.

The project follows a GeoAI workflow in which satellite spectral information is transformed into structured datasets for machine learning-based land cover prediction.

%------------------------------------------------
\section{Event Selection}
%------------------------------------------------

The selected case study corresponds to the Armero region in Tolima, Colombia, affected historically by the Nevado del Ruiz volcanic eruption.

\subsection{Reasons for Selection}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Historical and environmental relevance.
    \item Presence of heterogeneous land cover types.
    \item Availability of Sentinel-2 imagery.
    \item Suitable conditions for supervised classification tasks.
\end{itemize}

The analysis focuses on identifying present-day surface conditions and understanding the spatial distribution of land cover categories within the study area.

\subsection{Methodology Overview}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Download of Sentinel-2 Level-1C imagery from the Copernicus Browser for the Armero region.
    \item Stacking of individual spectral bands (JP2 format) into a single multi-band GeoTIFF using the \texttt{terra} package in R. Bands at 20~m resolution (B5, B6, B7, B8A, B11, B12) were resampled to 10~m to match the native resolution of B2, B3, B4, and B8.
    \item Digitization of training polygons in QGIS over the stacked image, using NDVI visualization to distinguish land cover classes.
    \item Extraction of pixel-level spectral values within training polygons using the \texttt{extract()} function from the \texttt{terra} package.
    \item Random sampling of 5,000 pixels to ensure computational efficiency while maintaining class representativeness.
    \item Export of the final dataset in Tab-Separated Values (TSV) format.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Expected Outcome}
%------------------------------------------------

The expected result of this first phase is the creation of a structured spectral dataset suitable for supervised machine learning training. This dataset will later be used to evaluate five classification architectures:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Decision Trees (DT)
    \item Support Vector Machines (SVM)
    \item Artificial Neural Networks (ANN)
    \item K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN)
    \item Naive Bayes (NB)
\end{itemize}

%------------------------------------------------
\section{Conclusion}
%------------------------------------------------

This first phase establishes the conceptual and technical foundation of the project by defining the study area, selecting the satellite imagery, and designing the methodology for spectral data extraction. The generated dataset will support future classification, validation, and thematic mapping tasks for the Armero region.
